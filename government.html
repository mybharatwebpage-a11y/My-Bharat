Government of India

The Government of the Republic of India is a federal parliamentary democratic republic, where the Prime Minister is the head of government and the President is the ceremonial head of state.

Central Government Structure

Executive

The executive branch is headed by the President, but real executive power lies with the Prime Minister and the Council of Ministers.

Legislative

India has a bicameral Parliament: the Lok Sabha (House of the People) and the Rajya Sabha (Council of States).

Judiciary

The Supreme Court of India is the highest court in the land, followed by High Courts in each state, ensuring constitutionality and justice.

Federalism

India is a federal republic, with powers shared between the central government and the state governments.

Central Ruling Party

Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP)

The BJP is the current ruling party at the Centre. Led by Prime Minister Narendra Modi, it focuses on nationalism, economic development, and governance reforms.
